About Us

ROMBLON TRANSPORT COOPERATIVE (RTC) was formed in compliance with the PUV modernization program. The existing local transport operators and van owners joined force to form the cooperative complying with the standards set by law.

RTC organized the association of Tourist, UV Express, Jeepney and Rental transport services in the Province of Romblon to provide quality transport services.

RTC aims to address transport problem and support the development and effort of the government to modernize transport system and ultimately bring about economic development and progress.

This Cooperative is organized to engage in:
  1. Provide transport services to members and commuting public;
  2. Engage in businesses such as:
    1. Sales of spare parts and supplies;
    2. Sales of Vehicle/Driver’s insurance policies;
    3. Auto Repair and Maintenance Shop;
    4. Vehicle Rental Services; and
    5. Micro Lending;
  3. Promote economic and social status of members;
  4. Coordinate and facilitate activities of cooperatives;
  5. Advocate for the cause of cooperative movements;
  6. Ensure viability of cooperatives through utilization of new technologies; and
  7. Encourage, promote self-help or self-employment for economic growth and poverty alleviation.

To help improve the quality life of its members and contribute to inclusive growth, enterprise development and employment. It shall aim:
  1. To attain increased income, savings, investments, productivity, and equitable distribution of surplus;
  2. To provide optimum social and economic benefits to its members;
  3. To teach members of doing things in a Cooperative manner;
  4. To propagate Cooperative practices;
  5. To empower through provision of opportunities to the poor and less privileged groups;
  6. To actively support the government, other Cooperatives and people-oriented organization;
  7. To develop a dynamic savings mobilization and capital build-up schemes;
  8. To adopt membership expansion scheme;
  9. To ensure transparency, accountability and promote the interests of the members;
  10. To adopt plans to foster the welfare of members, their families and the community;
  11. To advance the competitiveness and innovativeness of the industry;
  12. To coordinate with other Cooperatives on learning, trade and information exchanges;
  13. To advocate legal framework and policies for the development of transport Cooperatives; and
  14. To be the voice of the poor in resisting growth-centered development aggression.
